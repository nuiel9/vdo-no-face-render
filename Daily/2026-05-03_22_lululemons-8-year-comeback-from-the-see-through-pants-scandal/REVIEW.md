## Thesis

In March 2013, Lululemon voluntarily pulled roughly 17% of its women's yoga pants from sale after customers discovered the black luon fabric turned see-through in a downward dog position. The immediate cost was $17.5 million in gross profit. The deeper cost was structural: the recall exposed a founder-concentration governance architecture that had no institutional buffer between the company's public image and its founder's personal behavior. Over the next five years, Lululemon cycled through three CEOs, watched its stock fall roughly in half, and watched its founder exit the chairmanship following a live television interview that became the most-cited executive PR failure of 2013. The story most people know ends at the scandal. The one worth telling starts after it: how Lululemon grew net revenue from $1.591 billion in fiscal 2013 to $8.111 billion in fiscal 2022 without discounting a single pair of pants, and why the actual turning point was not a product fix but a governance separation.

## Contrarian Angles (5; selected marked)

1. **[SELECTED] The sheer pants were the surface; the root cause was founder-control governance dysfunction.** Wilson had retained structural board authority after stepping back from day-to-day operations, creating a company with no independent editorial layer between product quality, CEO authority, and founder public behavior. Three CEO transitions in five years (Day, Potdevin, McDonald) reflect a governance architecture that required Wilson's full exit to resolve. This angle fits Rise and Fall because the fall is not the product recall but the institutional failure underneath it, and the rise is explicitly locked behind the founder's departure from control.

2. **Brand resilience thesis.** Revenue grew 16% in the scandal year. A truly damaged brand contracts revenue. Lululemon's loyal customer base demonstrated that the scandal was a founder problem, not a product-category problem. The premium held.

3. **Holding price discipline during the crisis was the highest-value decision Lululemon made in 2013.** Competitors including Athleta and Gap's activewear lines could have permanently undercut Lululemon's pricing position during its vulnerability window. Lululemon never discounted. The implicit decision to hold price, not any product fix, preserved the margin structure that funded the comeback.

4. **Potdevin's rehabilitation role was systematically underreported.** Laurent Potdevin (CEO 2014-2018) is largely absent from the comeback narrative because of his conduct-related exit. But the men's category investment, international expansion into China and Europe, and DTC digital channel rebuild all began on his watch.

5. **Mirror ($500M, 2020) repeated the founder-control pattern in structural form.** A large capital bet on an unproven hardware adjacency, made without operational proof points, ultimately discontinued in 2023. The difference from 2013 was that the core business had sufficient scale to absorb the loss without governance impact.

## 6 Lesser-Known Facts with Citations

1. The luon recall was a supply chain failure, not a design defect: the fabric manufacturer had altered the weave density of Lululemon's proprietary luon blend without authorization, and Lululemon's internal QC process failed to catch the change before shipment. Source: Christine Day statements and contemporaneous WSJ coverage, March 2013. VERIFY exact supplier-changed-spec claim against primary record.

2. Lululemon's full-year FY2013 net revenue still grew to $1.591B (up 16% from $1.370B the prior year), despite the recall. The scandal slowed growth from 37% to 16%; it did not reverse it. Source: Lululemon 10-K FY2013, SEC EDGAR.

3. The yoga pants were not subject to a formal CPSC (Consumer Product Safety Commission) mandatory recall order. Lululemon voluntarily withdrew the product from sale. No CPSC recall entry appears in the CPSC database for Lululemon's 2013 luon bottoms. VERIFY via: https://www.cpsc.gov/Recalls

4. Chip Wilson's "some women's bodies" comment was made during a joint Bloomberg Television appearance with his wife Shannon, not a solo press conference. He was responding to a question about pilling, not sheer fabric. The context: a question about durability shifted into a comment about body type. Source: Bloomberg TV video archive, November 2013. VERIFY broadcast date.

5. Calvin McDonald, who led the most successful growth period in company history, came from Sephora Americas (not from activewear or athletic apparel). His prior background was luxury consumer retail operations. Source: Lululemon press release, August 2018 CEO appointment announcement. VERIFY exact appointment date and his Sephora title.

6. Lululemon's men's revenue crossed $1 billion in annual revenue for the first time in fiscal 2021. At the time of the 2013 recall, men's was a negligible segment. Source: Lululemon 10-K FY2021, SEC EDGAR. VERIFY exact men's segment revenue line.
