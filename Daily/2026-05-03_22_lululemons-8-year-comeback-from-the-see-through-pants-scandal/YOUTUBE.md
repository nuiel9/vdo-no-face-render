# YOUTUBE: The Yoga Pants That Nearly Destroyed a $10B Company
Row #22 | Lululemon | UTM: business-pm-22

In March 2013, Lululemon pulled 17% of its women's yoga pants from store shelves after customers discovered the black luon fabric turned see-through under tension. The $17.5 million gross profit hit was the visible crisis. The deeper damage was structural: founder Chip Wilson's on-camera comments about women's bodies forced him out of the chairmanship, and the company cycled through three CEOs in five years. This is the case study of a governance failure. Net revenue grew from $1.591 billion in FY2013 to $8.111 billion in FY2022. All claims sourced to SEC filings and primary records.
