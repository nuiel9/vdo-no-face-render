THUMBNAIL DESIGNER BRIEF
Row #2 — Costco's $1.50 Hot Dog
Template: Hidden Truth | Channel: Business Case Studies (Faceless)

---

CONCEPT (winner): "The Price Nobody Could Kill"

PRIMARY VISUAL ELEMENT:
A hot dog in a red tray, dramatically lit from one side as if under a spotlight. The $1.50 price tag is oversized — almost as tall as the hot dog itself — rendered in bold vintage yellow digits on a red background badge. The price tag should feel slightly absurd in scale, because that IS the joke: the number has not moved in 40 years.

CONTRAST ELEMENT:
Behind/beside the hot dog, a column of stacked coins or an inflation chart arrow pointing sharply upward (+185%), rendered as a faint infographic layer — not competing with the hero element but visible enough to create the tension.

TEXT OVERLAY (≤4 words):
"$1.50. Still. Why?"
— Sans-serif, white with black outline, bottom-right quadrant.

COLOR PALETTE:
#C8102E (Costco red), #F5A623 (price tag yellow), #1A1A2E (dark navy background for drama)

SILHOUETTE NOTE:
No generated likenesses of Jim Sinegal or Craig Jelinek. If a human figure is needed for scale, use a back-of-head or full silhouette only, camera-left, looking at the price tag.

MOBILE LEGIBILITY (320px check):
The $1.50 numeral must be readable at thumbnail size. Test at 120×68px before finalizing. The price badge should occupy at least 35% of the frame width.

FORMAT: 1280×720px, JPG, sRGB.
