## Description (100 words)

There is a government office inside the Department of Defense whose job is to review Hollywood screenplays and decide which productions qualify for military hardware access: aircraft carriers, fighter jets, forward operating bases. In exchange, studios agree to let the Pentagon approve the script. The governing instruction is DoDI 5410.16, publicly available and eight pages long. Researchers Tom Secker and Matthew Alford documented more than 800 films in this program using FOIA-obtained production agreements. This is not censorship. It is procurement. Both parties opt in. Most viewers never know the deal existed. Sources and FOIA database links in the description.
