# YOUTUBE — Row #2: Costco's $1.50 Hot Dog

---

## Video Description (100 words)

Since 1985, Costco has sold a hot dog and soda for $1.50. The price has never changed. In the same period, US consumer prices rose over 300%. This video breaks down why Costco didn't just hold the price — it built a factory, replaced its supplier, and encoded the decision into company culture passed from CEO to CEO. The answer isn't charity. It's that a $1.50 hot dog is worth more to Costco as an anti-churn behavioral anchor than as a food product. Sources: Costco 10-K FY2023 (SEC EDGAR), Craig Jelinek interviews, and Costco earnings call transcripts.
