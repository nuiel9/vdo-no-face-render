## VIDEO DESCRIPTION (100 words)

Costco has sold a hot dog and soda combo for $1.50 since 1985. In that same period, US inflation rose 185%. Most coverage stops at "the CEO threatened to fire anyone who raised the price." The primary-source record shows something far more deliberate: a company-owned factory built in California, a Nebraska chicken plant that follows the same playbook, and a membership fee engine generating $4.83 billion annually that funds the entire operation. This is not nostalgia. It is business architecture hiding in plain sight. Sources: Costco 10-K (SEC EDGAR), Jelinek/Seattle Times 2013, Costco Q1 FY2023 earnings call.

## PINNED COMMENT DRAFT

The part most people miss: Costco did not just *refuse* to raise the price. They built a factory. When Hebrew National costs got too high around 2008-2009, Costco launched its own hot dog production under Kirkland Signature — reportedly cutting per-unit cost by 30-40%. Then in 2019, the same playbook repeated for rotisserie chicken: they opened a chicken processing plant in Nebraska to hold the $4.99 price. The $1.50 hot dog is the headline. The supply chain is the story. Sources are in the description — CFO quote from the Dec 2022 earnings call is particularly worth reading in full.
