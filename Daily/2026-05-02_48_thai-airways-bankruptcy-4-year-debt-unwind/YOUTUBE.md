## Description (~100 words)

Thai Airways International filed for court rehabilitation on May 26, 2020 with 332 billion baht in total liabilities. But COVID was not the cause. THAI had been loss-making in five of the seven years before the pandemic, protected from restructuring by political interference no government was willing to end. What happened next was unusual for a flag carrier: the Thai government declined a bailout, removed THAI from the State Enterprise System, and let a court-supervised process force real creditor haircuts and genuine operational cuts. By FY2023, THAI reported a profit for the first time since 2017. This is the case study.

Sources: Thai Airways SET disclosures (ir.thaiairways.com) | Central Bankruptcy Court rehabilitation records | Thai Bankruptcy Act Section 90
